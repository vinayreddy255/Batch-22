Mother Teresa, born Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu in 1910 to Albanian parents in what is now southern Yugoslavia, is renowned for her profound humanitarian work. Raised in a devout Catholic household, she felt a calling to religious life from a young age. At eighteen, she joined the Loretto Sisters and moved to India, where she spent two decades teaching before receiving a transformative call to serve the poorest of the poor.
